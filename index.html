The Peanut Sorter and Grader project aims to streamline the process of sorting and grading peanuts efficiently. The process begins with breaking the groundnuts and removing any dust particles, which are vacuumed away. The peanuts are then transported along a conveyor belt, where they pass under a camera equipped with machine learning algorithms. This camera analyzes each peanut, distinguishing between good and bad ones based on predefined criteria. If a peanut is identified as bad, a servo mechanism is activated to redirect it for further processing. This could involve removing the defective peanut from the conveyor or subjecting it to corrective measures. Meanwhile, the good peanuts continue along the conveyor to the sorting and grading stage.
